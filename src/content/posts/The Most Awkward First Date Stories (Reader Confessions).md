We’ve all been there—that moment when you want the earth to open up and swallow you whole. The difference is, some of us are brave (or foolish) enough to share those moments with the world.

I asked readers to confess their most awkward first date experiences, and you delivered. Here are three stories that will make you cringe, laugh, and maybe feel a little better about your own dating disasters.

---

## 1. The Dog Who Ran Away With… Well, Everything

*— Anonymous, 28*

“I matched with this guy on Hinge, and we decided to meet at a park for a walk—low pressure, cute, right? I brought my dog, a very energetic golden retriever named Charlie, because the date was outdoors and I thought it would be a fun way to break the ice.

Everything was going great. We were laughing, the conversation flowed, Charlie was on his best behavior. Then, about 30 minutes in, Charlie spotted a squirrel. He lunged. The leash slipped out of my hand, and he took off—dragging my bag with him because, apparently, the leash had gotten tangled around the strap.

So there I was, chasing my dog through the park while my date stood there in shock. By the time I caught Charlie, my bag had opened and its contents were scattered across the grass. Including, I’m mortified to say, a box of tampons that rolled right to my date’s feet.

He picked it up, handed it to me, and said, ‘Well, that’s one way to break the ice.’

We actually dated for three months after that. Turns out, if a guy can handle your dog and your tampons on the first date, he might be a keeper.”

**Jadwiga’s take:** If a man can witness your tampon box rolling across the grass and still ask for a second date, marry him immediately.

## 2. The Accidental “I Love You”

*— Mark, 31*

“I’d been talking to this woman for a few weeks before we finally met in person. We hit it off instantly—great chemistry, lots of laughs. At the end of the night, we were saying goodbye, and I went in for a hug. But my brain, apparently exhausted from all the social interaction, decided to malfunction.

Instead of saying ‘It was great to meet you,’ I said, ‘I love you.’

Yes. *I love you.* On a first date. To a woman I barely knew.

The look on her face was pure horror. She just stared at me for a solid five seconds, then let out this awkward laugh and said, ‘Wow, okay, that’s… intense.’

I tried to backtrack: ‘No, no, I meant—I love *this*—I mean, I love *meeting* you—I love that we met—’ It just got worse. Eventually, I gave up, mumbled goodbye, and practically ran to my car.

I texted her later to apologize, and she said it was ‘the funniest first-date fail’ she’d ever experienced. She agreed to a second date, and we’ve been together for two years now.

**Jadwiga’s take:** The brain-mouth connection is fragile. Handle with care. But also, if someone can laugh off your verbal disaster, they’re probably a good match.

---

## 3. The Restaurant That Didn’t Exist

*— Chloe, 26*

“I matched with a guy on an app, and we decided to meet at this trendy new Italian place downtown. He picked it—said he’d been there before and the pasta was amazing. I showed up at 7 p.m., dressed up, excited. But when I got to the address, there was no restaurant. Just a shuttered storefront with a ‘For Lease’ sign.

I texted him: ‘Hey, are you sure this is the right place? I’m standing at the address but don’t see it.’

He replied: ‘Oh no. I just googled it—apparently it closed two months ago. I’m so sorry.’

So there we were, two strangers standing on opposite ends of the city, both hungry and embarrassed. He suggested we meet at a pizza place nearby, and I agreed. When I got there, he was already waiting, looking mortified. We ended up having a great time—the pizza was good, the conversation was better—and we laughed about the whole thing.

We’re still together, and every anniversary we go to that pizza place.”

**Jadwiga’s take:** Sometimes the best dates are the ones that go completely off-script. Flexibility is sexy.

---

## The Moral of the Stories

If there’s one thing these confessions teach us, it’s that **perfection is overrated**. The most memorable dates aren’t the ones where everything goes smoothly—they’re the ones where things go wrong, and you see how the other person handles it.

Can they laugh at themselves? Can they roll with the punches? Do they still want to see you again after you’ve accidentally declared your love or unleashed a tampon bomb?

That’s the real test.
