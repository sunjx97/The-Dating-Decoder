We’ve all had dates that didn’t go as planned. But some dates? They go so wrong they become legends.

I asked readers to share their most cringe-worthy dating moments. Here are four stories that prove no matter how bad your date was… someone else has had it worse.

---

## 1. The Lookalike Situation

*— Megan, 29*

“I matched with a guy on an app, and we talked for two weeks before meeting. Great conversations, similar sense of humor, all the right signs. When we finally agreed to meet at a coffee shop, I was genuinely excited.

I walked in, scanned the room, and spotted him at a corner table. He looked exactly like his photos—handsome, well-dressed, smiling. I waved, walked over, and said, ‘Hey! So great to finally meet you!’

He stared at me blankly. ‘I’m sorry,’ he said. ‘Do I know you?’

Turns out, the guy I was supposed to meet was sitting at the table *right behind him*. They had the same haircut, same jacket, same general vibe. I had just introduced myself to a complete stranger who was waiting for his own date.

I wanted to disappear. I apologized profusely while the actual date watched the whole thing from the next table. He thought it was hilarious. We ended up dating for six months.”

**The lesson:** Always confirm the exact table before approaching. Or at least check the name on their coffee cup.

---

## 2. The Ex Who Wouldn’t Leave

*— Daniel, 34*

“I met a woman at a friend’s party and asked her out. We picked a wine bar in a neighborhood neither of us lived in—neutral territory, seemed safe.

Twenty minutes into the date, a woman walks in, sees me, and marches straight to our table. It was my ex-girlfriend. The one who had broken up with me three months earlier. The one who had not taken the breakup well.

She pulled up a chair and sat down. ‘Oh, don’t mind me,’ she said. ‘I just want to see who Daniel’s moved on to.’

My date looked at me. I looked at my date. The ex just sat there, smiling.

I had to say, ‘I’m so sorry, but I need to handle this.’ I asked my date if we could reschedule, walked my ex out, and spent the next hour explaining that no, this was not okay.

My date gave me a second chance. We’ve been together two years now. The ex still sends me passive-aggressive texts.”

**The lesson:** Sometimes your past shows up uninvited. How you handle it tells your date everything.

---

## 3. The Spaghetti Incident

*— Priya, 27*

“I was on a second date at a nice Italian place. The first date had gone really well, so I was nervous but excited. I ordered spaghetti with marinara sauce—big mistake.

I took a bite, and somehow a piece of spaghetti flipped up and landed *on my date’s shirt*. A white shirt. With red sauce.

I froze. He looked down. There was a moment of silence where I genuinely considered crawling under the table and never emerging.

Then he picked up the spaghetti strand, ate it, and said, ‘Best table service I’ve ever had.’

We’ve been married for three years. He still tells this story at parties.”

**The lesson:** If someone can laugh with you through a food disaster, they’re probably a keeper.

---

## 4. The Wrong Name

*— Tom, 31*

“I’d been on three dates with a woman named Sarah. Things were going well—good conversations, clear chemistry. On the fourth date, we were at a bar, and I introduced her to a friend who happened to be there.

‘This is Sarah,’ I said.

She gave me a weird look. After my friend left, she said, ‘My name is Emma.’

Turns out, I had been calling her the wrong name for four dates. Not once did she correct me because she thought I was doing it intentionally? Or testing her? I still don’t understand.

I apologized about fifty times. She laughed it off, but I could tell it stung. We went on two more dates after that, but something had shifted. Eventually, we faded out.

I still cringe when I think about it.”

**The lesson:** Learn their name. Double-check it. Write it down if you have to. Names matter.

---

## What These Stories Teach Us

Awkward moments happen. They’re part of dating. What matters isn’t avoiding them—it’s how you and your date handle them.

Can you laugh together? Can you move past it? Can you see the human behind the embarrassment?

Sometimes the dates that go wrong end up being the best stories. And sometimes they lead to the best relationships.

Here’s to surviving the awkward moments—and finding someone who stays through them.
